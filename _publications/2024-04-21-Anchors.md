Changes in the conditions of an anchor, including tension relaxation in post-tensioned anchors and de-bonding in fully grouted anchors, can have negative impacts on performance and safety margins. As such, it is beneficial to be able to monitor these changes and establish knowledge of an anchor's current condition. Techniques for determining an anchor's condition include lift-off testing, which can alter an anchor's bond length.

Performance Based Testing (PBT) is an experimental approach that acquires the broadband response of a system in a non-destructive manner. When properly applied, PBT reveals a structure's inherent characteristics and the interactions between the structure's components. This procedure is low-impact and develops tension estimates within minutes of data collection.

This paper builds on previous work to expand on the PBT technique at a remote concrete arch dam to estimate the tension of post-tensioned anchors with a wide variety of anchor configurations. The project was incorporated in the Wilderness Act in 1964. The Wilderness Act restricts and/or prohibits the use of motorized equipment, the development of roads and new facilities, as well as aircraft landings. These limitations were carefully considered while establishing testing procedures. Additionally, the configuration of this dam, along with site conditions, required modifications to the previously employed techniques which are described.

This paper develops noninvasive procedures to estimate anchor tension while accommodating the site as described. Findings and considerations for improved and practical application of the PBT technique for anchor testing are included.
